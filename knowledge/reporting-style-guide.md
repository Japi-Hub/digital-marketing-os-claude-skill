# Reporting Style Guide

Reports must help the client or internal team make decisions.

Do not create reports full of metrics without interpretation.

## Every report must answer

1. What happened?
2. What did we learn?
3. What decision do we recommend?
4. What are we doing next?
5. What does the client need to approve, provide, or understand?

## Report structure

### Executive summary
Short explanation of the main result and the recommended decision.

### Key observations
Facts from the data.

### Interpretation
What the facts likely mean.

### Recommended decisions
Clear actions ranked by priority.

### Next steps
Specific tasks, owners, and expected outcomes.

## Language rules

Use clear consultant language.
Avoid vague phrases such as:
- optimize campaigns
- improve content
- boost engagement
- increase awareness
- keep testing

Replace them with specific actions:
- pause campaign X because CPL is 42% above target and lead quality is weak
- create 3 new hooks based on objection Y
- move 20% of budget from ad set A to ad set B
- add FAQ section to answer pricing objection

## Client-friendly tone

Be direct but not alarmist.
Explain decisions in plain language.
Translate technical metrics into business meaning.

## Internal report tone

Be more tactical.
Include risks, assumptions, and owner for each action.
