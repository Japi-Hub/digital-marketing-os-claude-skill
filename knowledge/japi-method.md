# Método JAPI

WhatsApp is not a catalog. It is a guided sales conversation.

The seller's job is not to answer messages. The seller's job is to guide the client toward a decision.

## Core principles

- Start by understanding the objective of WhatsApp.
- Connect before selling.
- Guide the conversation instead of reacting to the client.
- Ask questions that move the conversation forward.
- Avoid sending price without context.
- Avoid long monologues.
- Avoid generic replies.
- Use clear next steps.
- Use binary CTAs when possible.

## Common WhatsApp sales problems

### The informant
Only gives information. Does not guide.

### The ghost
Does not follow up or disappears.

### The monologue
Sends too much text and creates cognitive overload.

### The robot
Sounds automated, cold, or generic.

### The passive seller
Waits for the client to decide alone.

### The rushed seller
Sends price too fast without context.

### The distracted seller
Does not connect the client's need with the offer.

### The improviser
Has no structure or next step.

## Conversation analysis checklist

Look for:
- Where the lead cooled down
- Where the seller lost control
- Whether the seller understood the client's problem
- Whether the seller guided toward a decision
- Whether the CTA was clear
- Whether the answer felt like a catalog
- Whether the client received too much information too soon
- Whether the follow-up added value

## Strong follow-up behavior

A strong follow-up does not say only: “Quería dar seguimiento.”

It should add one of these:
- reminder of value
- case mirror
- proof
- deadline
- helpful clarification
- low-friction next step

## Output rule

When analyzing WhatsApp, always connect the insight to:
- sales script improvement
- content opportunity
- ad angle
- landing page improvement
- follow-up asset
