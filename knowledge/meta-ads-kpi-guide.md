# Meta Ads KPI Guide

## Core metrics

CTR = Link Clicks / Impressions
CPC = Spend / Link Clicks
CPM = Spend / Impressions * 1000
CPA = Spend / Conversions
CPL = Spend / Leads
CAC = Spend / Customers
ROAS = Revenue / Spend
CVR = Conversions / Clicks

## Diagnosis rules

### High CPM may indicate
- Audience too narrow
- Competitive market
- Weak relevance
- Poor creative-market fit
- Seasonality

### Low CTR may indicate
- Weak hook
- Wrong audience
- Unclear offer
- Creative fatigue
- Poor first frame
- Message mismatch

### Good CTR but bad leads may indicate
- Curiosity clicks
- Weak qualification
- Offer mismatch
- Landing page disconnect
- WhatsApp handoff problem

### Good leads but bad sales may indicate
- Slow response
- Weak follow-up
- Sales conversation problem
- Pricing objection
- Poor next step
- Lead quality issue

### High frequency and declining CTR may indicate
- Creative fatigue
- Audience saturation
- Need for new hooks, angles, or formats

### Low CPL but low close rate means
The campaign is not necessarily successful. It may be generating cheap but low-quality leads.

## Decision logic

Recommend `scale carefully` when:
- CPA/CPL is at or below target
- Lead quality is acceptable
- Conversion rate is stable
- Frequency is not too high
- Creative still has healthy CTR

Recommend `maintain` when:
- Performance is stable
- There is not enough evidence to scale
- Metrics are acceptable but not outstanding

Recommend `reduce budget` when:
- Cost is rising
- Quality is dropping
- Frequency is high
- There are better-performing alternatives

Recommend `pause` when:
- Spend is significant
- Results are poor
- CTR is weak
- CPA/CPL is above acceptable range
- There is no strategic learning left

Recommend `refresh creative` when:
- Frequency is high
- CTR is declining
- CPC is increasing
- Comments or reactions suggest fatigue

Recommend `investigate funnel` when:
- Ads generate leads but sales do not close
- Landing conversion is weak
- WhatsApp response or follow-up is slow
- Lead quality is unclear

## Reporting rule

Never report metrics alone. Always translate metrics into a decision.
