# Content Intelligence Framework

Organic content must be evaluated by business objective, not by vanity metrics only.

## Content objectives

### Attraction
Useful metrics:
- reach
- views
- retention
- shares
- new profile visits

Purpose:
Bring new people into the ecosystem.

### Education
Useful metrics:
- saves
- comments
- watch time
- completion rate
- replies

Purpose:
Help the audience understand the problem, opportunity, or solution.

### Trust
Useful metrics:
- saves
- replies
- comments with intent
- shares to peers
- profile visits

Purpose:
Build credibility and reduce perceived risk.

### Conversion
Useful metrics:
- DMs
- link clicks
- leads
- booked calls
- purchases
- WhatsApp conversations

Purpose:
Move people toward a commercial decision.

### Remarketing
Useful metrics:
- replies from warm audience
- clicks from warm audience
- objections resolved
- sales conversations reactivated

Purpose:
Convert people who already know the brand.

## Analysis questions

For each content report, identify:
- Which topics attracted attention?
- Which topics created commercial intent?
- Which hooks performed best?
- Which formats performed best?
- Which CTAs generated action?
- Which content should become ads?
- Which ads should become organic content?
- Which objections need new content?
- Which sales conversations reveal content gaps?

## Pattern categories

Tag content by:
- topic
- hook style
- format
- funnel stage
- CTA
- audience segment
- objection addressed
- offer mentioned

## Recommended output

A content analysis should produce:
1. What worked
2. What did not work
3. What we learned
4. What to repeat
5. What to stop
6. What to test next
7. Suggested content calendar
8. Content-to-ad opportunities
9. Ad-to-content opportunities

## Decision rule

Do not recommend more content. Recommend the next right content based on evidence.
